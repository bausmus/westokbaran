Software Development

Knowledge/Skills

Software development is a very popular career today, with so many avenues to choose from for your profession. Among those, there are careers in back-end development, front-end
development, and full-stack develpment that cover most job opportunities out there. Back-end development primarily covers the logic side of developing websites or desktop/mobile
applications. Basically, it is programming the logic that will work with moving data around and handling requests from the user, as well as CRUD operations. Front-end development
mainly deals with the user interface (UI). So, it would be programming the views that users look at, user interactions, and front end web and application operations. Lastly, full-stack
development covers both back-end, front-end, and also the API layer. Full-stack development has become the most in demand career for companies to choose for hiring developers.

Training/Education

Becoming a software developer can be challenging, but there are many ways to become a developer. There are many people who grew up learning to code at school or on their own as a
hobby, and have become great developers in the process. Many employers are willing to hire people without formal training if they show their proficiency to code and design applications.
Another route to becoming a software engineer is to obtain formal training by going to college to obtain a degree in computer sceince, informaiton technology, computer engineering,
engineering, or mathematics. Employers like to see that people have put in the time to learn all the desired skills for the profession. Lastly, another way to get the needed training
is to enroll in a coding bootcamp, technology center, or online program to obtain certifications for completing software development training. This shows employers that you put in the
necessary work to develop your skills as a developer.
